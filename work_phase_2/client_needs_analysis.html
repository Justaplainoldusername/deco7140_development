WP2 - Client Needs Analysis

The Client Brief

About Us

We are a non-profit organisation dedicated to fostering social cohesion by empowering diverse communities to share their stories, connect with others, and access resources that enhance their visibility and influence. Our mission is to strengthen societal bonds by creating platforms that celebrate and support the unique identities of communities across Australia.

This brief has been prepared for the internal design teams at Connect Communities Initiative (CCI). Each design team is tasked with developing individual concepts and prototypes for dynamic websites that align with CCI’s mission and vision. Our mission is to strengthen societal bonds by creating platforms that celebrate and support the unique identities of communities across Australia.

Project Overview

We are commissioning the development of dynamic, user-friendly websites that represent and support different communities. Each website should serve as a central hub where community members can share resources, highlight events, and foster meaningful connections. The websites must not include any form of commercial activity, such as advertising or selling products. However, it is acceptable to promote resources, events, or services that are directly beneficial to the community.

The websites must be accessible, mobile-friendly, and reflect the diverse needsof the selected community focus area while adhering to our mission of inclusivity, accessibility, and ethical design. Each website should also celebrate the unique characteristics of its chosen community and support meaningful interaction and engagement.

Community Focus Areas

  • Cultural and Heritage Communities
  • Academic and Professional Communities
  • Wellness and Mental Health Communities
  • Hobbies and Sub-Cultures
  • Social and Environmental Advocacy Communities
  • Regional or International Communities
  • Creative Arts and Media Communities
  • Lived Experience Communities
  • Food and Culinary Communities
  • Tech and Innovation Communities

Additional Information

Target Audience

Each website must serve two key groups:

  1. Community Members: Individuals within the selected community who seek representation, resources, and connection.
  2. General Audience: Broader society, who can use the website to learn about the community and its contributions.

Accessibility must be prioritised to ensure seamless interaction for all users, including those relying on assistive technologies.

Project Goals

  1. Represent and celebrate the identity of the chosen community, recognising and representing the diversity within the community rather than relying on stereotypes.
  2. Provide clear, accessible information and resources to users.
  3. Foster meaningful connections and support within the community.
  4. Ensure ethical, inclusive, and culturally sensitive design principles are upheld.

Functional Requirements

Regardless of the chosen community focus, the website must include:

  1. Response Design: Use a mobile-first approach to create a responsive, user-friendly design across devices, with support for Progressive Web App (PWA) functionality to ensure compatibility across smartphones and desktop/laptops.
  2. Accessible Design:Meet WCAG 2.2 Level AA standards, ensuring that navigation, text alternatives, and other features are accessible to all users.
  3. Dynamic ContentIntegrate at least one interactive or dynamic feature, from the following options:
    • Resource directorywhere community members can POST information that is relevant to the community. Resources may be for example, recipes, reviews of travel destinations, tips for supporting mental health, etc.
    • Events directory: where community members can POST events for other members to see.
    • Discussion board: a simple discussion board where community members can interact with each other.
    • Content ratings: a rating system where users can rate webpage content (including dynamic content) and average ratings are then available.

APIs for Dynamic Content

APIs have been developed by the CCI backend team to support these dynamic features, and you must use these APIs exclusively. No alternative APIs or external data sources may be used in your implementation.

Additional Requirement - Newsletter Subscription Feature

As part of each website development, we want you to integrate a third-party newsletter subscription plug-in provided by one of our funders. This plug-in is designed to allow users to subscribe to newsletters from the funder. When subscribing, users will be asked to complete the following information:

  • Full name
  • Email address
  • Occupation
  • Annual income
  • Household size
  • Sexuality
  • Gender
  • Spending habits
  • Type of phone they currently use
  • Monthly spend on dining out/takeaway food
  • Number of credit cards they have with the credit limit on each card

Values - Needs - Features Analysis

Value 1: Representation

The client values representation. Their goal is provide a website that represents the chosen community.

Unique characteristics

Feature

A page dedicated to promoting the unique characteristics of the group.

Learn about the community

Feature

A page for the general public to learn about the community and read answers to commonly asked questions.

Diversity within the community

Feature

Value 2: Connection

The client values connection. Their goal is to create a website that enables connection within the chosen community.

Share resources

Feature

A resources directory for users to share, access and download resources.

Meaningful connections, interaction and engagement

Feature

A discussion board for users to post and answer questions.

Highlight events

Feature

A section on the discussion board for users to post and answer questions.

Value 3: Inclusivity

The client values inclusivity. Their goal is to create a space that is inclusive to all, including non-community members.

Accessible

Feature

Adherance to the WCAG2.2 Level AA standards.

Mobile-friendly

Feature

A mobile-first approach with support for Progressive Web App (PWA) functionality.

Ethical design

Feature

Design will be culturally-sensitive, contain only necessary feature, and either create good or minimise harm.
