Reflective Design

"Good reflective design makes values visible." Here you will discover how we have used the values of you and your community to inform the images, language and tone that will encourage long-term attachment to your website.

Representation

How we represent the unique characteristics and diversity of the community is important for both the community, who wish to see themselves in your website, and people outside of the community who wish to learn about the community from an accurate source.

Images

The boat building community is diverse with members from all age groups, genders and ethnicities. We will represent the diversity of your community through carefully selected images that allow every community member to see themselves represented in your website.

Language and Tone

Language choice and tone is an important aspect of community representation. We recommend keeping the language of the site neutral in terms of gender, age and ethnic background, friendly and welcoming, and practical, much like the language used in a boat building kit. We shall focus on what unites the community, rather than segments it, by referring to our members as "Our community", "Our builder community", or "our builders".

When referring to an identifiable individual, such as an historical figure or a contributer, we shall use their chosen or documented pronouns. Where there is ambiguity, we shall use the gender neutral they/them.

While it may be tempting to include nautical clich√©s such as "Ahoy Matey" in our text, we respect that our community is diverse with many hailing from non-nautical backgrounds. While some may indulge in puns and clich√©s, others may find this tone restrictive and unrepresentative of their experience of boat building. For those individuals that wish to express their appreciation for nautical clich√©s, the interactive elements of the site, such as the forums and comments sections, and the community lead Stories section will provide these opportunities and their unique voices will contribute to the rich diversity of the website while still provided a neutral platform for the other voices within the community to be heard.

Connection

Even though model boat building is generally considered a solo hobby, our community have communicated their desire to connect with others, and the pleasure they experience when they do.

Images

Regularly including images of groups of people partaking in model boat building throughout the website will reiterate our value of Connection and reinforce that boat building needn't be a solo pursuit.

Inclusivity

It is important to use that we create a website that is inclusive to all. This means ensuring accessibility, ethical design, and ease-of-use across all platforms and devices.

Language and Tone

We anticipate that our community will have a range of reading levels and proficiency with the English language. To ensure our website is inclusive of all, we recommend using language that written clearly and simply. According to WCAG 2.2 Success Criterion 3.1.5, this is considered to be at a reading level at or below lower secondary education level (generally 7-9 years of school).

Where language exceeds that of lower secondary, users must be able to acquire a simplified version of the text. This may be relevant where community explanations are pitched to fellow experts, or when community Stories are targetted toward a high level of sophistication with English.

Sensitive Terms

We appreciate that the language of history has not always been sensitive to diversity, with sexism, racism and ableism often playing key roles in historic events. Our community have expressed interest in the historical accuracy of nautical-life and there will, on occasion, be terms used that are historically accurate for the era that would be considered insensitive or offensive today. To allow for historically accurate expression while still being sensitive to the diverse community, texts containing such terms or ideas will be annotated to provide a forewarning to concerned readers, historical context and reiterate our modern values of inclusivity and respect of diversity.

We hope that in taking this approach we not only show respect, but help to educate our community on the views of the past that may still impact on the quality of lives of people today.

Forewarning Example

Forewarning: This article contains historical terms or phrases that are now considered offensive to the [nation/community]. For historical accuracy we have annotated these terms with explanations and links to further information. We encourage sensitive individuals to proceed through this story with caution.

Text excerpt

"Captain's Log, August 9, 1878: With the [derogatory term]* safely onboard, we set sail across the coral sea bound for the Port of Mackay."

Annotation Example

*The term [derogatory term] was used to represent the [people from nation/community] and is now considered offensive by modern standards. We pay respect to those from the [nation/community] whose lives have been impacted by the ideas and values of the past. To learn more about this aspect of history, and help right the wrongs of the past visit [helpful link].

Consistent Layout and UI Behaviour

Wherever elements have the same appearance they will share the same behaviour. For example, if a feature looks like a card, then it will display all the same characteristics and functionality of other cards on the site, such as hover effects and hyperlinking.

In the example, the second card does not have the same hover or focus features of the first, but when clicked on still operates as a hyperlink. This behaviour is confusing and should be avoided.

Consistency in layout and UI behaviour enables your website to meet WCAG 2.2 Success Criterion 3.2.4.

User Interactivity and Feedback

Where users will be interacting with the website, such as through forms, error identification and error suggestion will be implemented to meet the WCAG 2.2 Success Criterion 3.3.1 and 3.3.3.

To experience this, attempt to submit the form without completing the Full Name field, or alternatively, try inputting an invalid email address into the Email field. Error messages and error suggestions will appear to assist the users to successfully submit the form.

Non-Commercial

One of the clearly stated requirements for your website is to maintain it as a non-commercial space. This value is supported by the community, who have expressed need for discretion surrounding the costs associated with the hobby.

Images

All images should be free from indicators of cost (such as price tags) and preferably brand, although we accept that certain brands will be preferred by users and recommended in the Discussion Board or other Resources.

Language and Tone

Wherever possible, language is to be brand neutral so as not to create the perception of brand preference or alliance. Situations where this does not apply includes references to specific kits by make and model for the purposes of collating kit specific resources, and in the Discussion Board where users are welcome to express their preference for certain brands so long as their interest is not commercial.

References to costs or values should be avoided, however advice on how to reduce costs, such a tool or material alternatives, is strongly encouraged.
