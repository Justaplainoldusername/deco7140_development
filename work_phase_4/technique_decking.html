Decking

Decking can either be a joy or a pain, depending on your technique and materials. Here we provide our advice on approaching this fiddly but rewarding aspect of the build.

1. Prepare the Hull

Ensure the hull is fully sanded smooth and symmetrical. Mark the deck’s position on the hull using the plans as a guide. Dry-fit any bulkheads or beams that will support the deck to check alignment.

2. Fit the Deck Beams and Subdeck

Glue the deck beams and any supports firmly into place. Add the subdeck (usually a thin plywood sheet) and clamp or pin it while the glue dries. Once dry, sand it smooth and level—it forms the base for your planking.

3. Plan the Planking Layout

Mark the centerline and plank runs on the subdeck. Decide on plank width, length, and any joggling or nibbing near the edges. Pre-cut and taper planks where necessary to match the ship’s curves.

4. Lay the Planks

Start from the centerline and work outward on both sides for symmetry. Use wood glue sparingly to prevent staining. Stagger butt joints for a realistic look. Clamp or pin each plank until the glue sets.

5. Sand and Seal

Once all planks are laid, sand the deck smooth and even. Remove any excess glue or filler. Apply a clear sealer, varnish, or light oil to protect the wood and bring out the grain.
